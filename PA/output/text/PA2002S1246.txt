24  Section 102.  Definitions.
1     The following words and phrases when used in this act shall
2  have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
3  context clearly indicates otherwise:
4     "Applicant."  An individual who applies to be registered to
5  vote as provided for in this act.
6     "Calendar year."  The period commencing January 1 and ending
7  December 31 next following.
8     "Commission."  A registration commission established under
9  section 303.
10     "Commissioner."  A member of a registration commission.
11     "County."  Any county of this Commonwealth. The term includes
12  a county within which is located a city of the first class or
13  with which a city of the first class is coextensive.
14     "County election board."  The board of elections of a county.
15     "Department."  The Department of State of the Commonwealth.
16     "District."  An election district or precinct of a
17  municipality.
18     "District register."  The [registration cards or digitized
19  signature list containing the] list of qualified electors
20  containing the digitized signature of each elector:
21         (1)  arranged, by election district, alphabetically by
22     last name of the registrant; and
23         (2)  prepared by the commission under section 702.
24     "Election."  A general, special, municipal or primary
25  election.
26     "General election."  The election which the Constitution of
27  Pennsylvania requires to be held in even-numbered years.
28     "General register."  The [registration record of a county
29  containing at a minimum all information required on a
30  registration application and a registration card for all
1  registered electors in the county.] data base of all
2  registration records appropriate to a county contained on a
3  computer in accordance with section 701.
4     "In military service."  Serving in the uniformed services as
5  defined in section 102 of the Career Compensation Act of 1949
6  (Public Law 81-350, 63 Stat. 801) or serving in the Pennsylvania
7  National Guard.
8     "Members of the merchant marine of the United States."  Any
9  of the following:
10         (1)  Individuals employed as officers or members of crews
11     of vessels documented under the law of the United States or
12     of vessels owned by the United States or of vessels of
13     foreign-flag registry under charter to or control of the
14     United States. This paragraph does not include individuals in
15     military service.
16         (2)  Individuals enrolled with the United States for
17     employment or for training for employment or maintained by
18     the United States for emergency relief service as officers or
19     members of crews of vessels referred to in paragraph (1). The
20     term does not include individuals in military service or
21     individuals employed or enrolled for employment or for
22     training for employment or maintained for emergency relief on
23     the Great Lakes or the inland waterways.
24     "Military elector."  Any of the following:
25         (1)  An individual in military service and the
26     individual's spouse and dependents.
27         (2)  An individual in the merchant marine and the
28     individual's spouse and dependents.
29         (3)  An individual in a religious or welfare group
30     officially attached to and serving with the armed forces of
1     the United States and the individual's spouse and dependents.
2         (4)  An individual who is a civilian employee of the
3     United States outside the territorial limits of the United
4     States, whether or not the individual is subject to the civil
5     service laws and the Classification Act of 1949 (Public Law
6     81-429, 63 Stat. 954) and whether or not the individual is
7     paid from funds appropriated by Congress, and the
8     individual's spouse and dependents.
9     "Municipal election."  The election which the Constitution of
10  Pennsylvania requires to be held in odd-numbered years.
11     "Municipality."  A city, borough, town or township.
12     "November election."  Either the general or the municipal
13  election, or both, according to the context.
14     "Overseas citizen."  An individual who resides outside the
15  territorial limits of the United States and who, but for such
16  residence, would be a qualified elector. The term does not
17  include a military elector.
18     "Party."  Any of the following:
19         (1)  A party or political body, one of whose candidates
20     at the general election immediately preceding the primary:
21             (i)  polled, in each of at least ten counties, at
22         least 2% of the largest entire vote cast in the county
23         for any elected candidate; and
24             (ii)  polled a total vote in this Commonwealth equal
25         to at least 2% of the largest entire vote cast in this
26         Commonwealth for any elected candidate.
27         (2)  A party or political body, one of whose candidates
28     at either the general or municipal election preceding the
29     primary polled at least 5% of the largest entire vote cast
30     for any elected candidate in any county.
1     "Person authorized to administer oaths."  The term includes
2  an individual who is a commissioned officer in military service
3  or a member of the merchant marine of the United States
4  designated for this purpose by the United States Secretary of
5  Commerce.
6     "Person in military service."  The term shall mean a
7  qualified elector who is by enlistment, enrollment or draft in
8  actual military service.
9     "Political body."  A political body not recognized as a
10  political party which has filed proper nomination papers as
11  required by law.
12     "Primary election."  An election for the nomination of
13  candidates.
14     "Public office."  Any Federal, State or political
15  subdivision, office or position of employment requiring the
16  individual elected or appointed to render public service for a
17  fixed fee or compensation. The term does not include the office
18  of notary public or commissioner of deeds.
19     "Qualified elector."  An individual who possesses all of the
20  qualifications for voting prescribed by the Constitution of
21  Pennsylvania and the laws of this Commonwealth or who, being
22  otherwise qualified by continued residence in the election
23  district, obtains such qualifications before the next ensuing
24  election. The term does not include a military elector.
25     "Registrant."  An elector who is registered to vote in
26  accordance with this act.
27     ["Registration card."  A registration record containing all
28  information required on the registration application, including
29  the elector's signature, and suitable space for the insertion by
30  the appropriate official of the following information:
1         (1)  The ward and election district of residence.
2         (2)  The registrant's street address.
3         (3)  Data required to be given upon removal from the
4     registrant's residence.
5         (4)  The date of each election at which the registrant
6     votes.
7         (5)  The number and letter of the stub of the ballot
8     issued to the registrant or the registrant's number in the
9     order of admission to the voting machines.
10         (6)  The initials of the election officer who enters the
11     record of voting in the district register.
12         (7)  Whether the registrant needs assistance to vote and,
13     if so, the nature of the disability.]
14     "Registration [records] record."  The [general register,
15  district register and any other record of registration of
16  electors maintained by a county. If provided in regulations
17  promulgated under this act, the term shall also refer to any
18  record maintained by the county on the Statewide Uniform
19  Registry of Electors.] registration information of each
20  registrant contained in the general register of the county.
21     "Registry."  The Statewide Uniform Registry of Electors
22  (SURE) of registered qualified electors established under
23  section 322.
24     "Secretary."  The Secretary of the Commonwealth.
25     "Statewide Uniform Registry of Electors" or "SURE system."
26  The integrated voter registration system containing a data base

28  Section 301.  Departmental responsibilities.
29     The department shall do all of the following:
30         (1)  Provide for applicants to submit their voter
1     registration application to the commission, the Department of
2     Transportation and other agencies designated in section 525.
3         (2)  Prescribe a procedure for the return of completed
4     voter registration applications from the Department of
5     Transportation, the Department of Public Welfare, armed
6     forces recruitment centers, Offices of the Clerk of Orphan's
7     Court and all other offices under this act to the secretary
8     or the appropriate commission.
9         (3)  Develop [and], establish and administer a Statewide
10     Uniform Registry of Electors to be known as the SURE system,
11     subject to the requirements of [section 321] this act.
12         (4)  Promulgate regulations necessary to administer this
13     act.
14         (5)  Conduct annual training of all State and county
15     officials and personnel responsible for using the SURE system
16     and administering the voter registration laws of this
17     Commonwealth; offer such training as often as necessary but
18     not less than once per year; and provide a training manual.
19     The department shall issue a certification to all persons who
20     complete such training.
21         (6)  The department shall prepare from the SURE system
22     within 20 days after the last day to register to vote at each
23     special, primary, municipal and general election, a statement
24     of the total number of electors registered in each election
25     district, together with a breakdown of registration by each
26     political party or other designation. The department shall
27     forthwith submit such information to the Legislative Data
28     Processing Center and shall publicly report the total number
29     of registered electors for each political party or other
30     designation in each county and in each election district not
1     later than five days prior to the primary, municipal or
2     general election.
3         (7)  Upon request the department shall prepare a
4     statement of the total number of electors within each
5     political party or other designation registered in each
6     Congressional, Senatorial and legislative district or other
7     political subdivision. Such reports shall be made available
8     to the General Assembly, on request, and to the public for a
9     fee, as established by the department.
10         (8)  The department shall maintain and prepare upon
11     request from the SURE system, maps of each election district
12     of the Commonwealth as well as each Congressional, Senatorial
13     or legislative district or other political subdivision. Such
14     maps shall be made available to the General Assembly, on
15     request, and to the public for a fee, as established by the
16     department.

21  assistant or commission employee may do any of the following:
22         (1)  Intentionally insert or permit to be inserted an
23     entry in a registration [card] record without a proper
24     application under this act or without requiring the proper
25     evidence of the right of the applicant to be registered.

27     record after the entries have been made. This paragraph does
28     not apply to an alteration pursuant to an order of a court of
29     common pleas or of the commission.

1  misdemeanor of the first degree and shall, upon conviction, be
2  sentenced to pay a fine of not more than $10,000 or to
3  imprisonment for not more than five years, or both.
