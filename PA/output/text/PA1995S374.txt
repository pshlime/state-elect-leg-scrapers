12                             CHAPTER 1
13                       PRELIMINARY PROVISIONS
14  Section 101.  Short title.
15     This act shall be known and may be cited as the Pennsylvania
16  Voter Registration Act.
17  Section 102.  Definitions.
18     The following words and phrases when used in this act shall
19  have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
20  context clearly indicates otherwise:
21     "Calendar year."  The period commencing the first day of
22  January and ending the thirty-first day of December next
23  following.
24     "Commission."  A registration commission established under
25  section 303.
26     "Commissioner."  A member of a registration commission.
27     "County."  Any county of this Commonwealth. The term includes
28  a county within which is located a city of the first class or
29  with which a city of the first class is coextensive.
30     "County election board."  The county board of elections of a
1  county.
2     "Department."  The Department of State of the Commonwealth.
3     "District."  An election district or precinct of a
4  municipality.
5     "District register."  The registration cards or digitized
6  signature list containing the list of qualified electors:
7         (1)  arranged, by election district, alphabetically by
8     last name of the registrant; and
9         (2)  prepared by a commission under section 702.
10     "Election."  A general, special, municipal or primary
11  election.
12     "General election."  The election which the Constitution of
13  Pennsylvania requires to be held in even-numbered years.
14     "In military service."  Serving in the uniformed services as
15  defined in section 102 of the Career Compensation Act of 1949
16  (Public Law 81-350, 63 Stat. 801) or serving in the Pennsylvania
17  National Guard.
18     "Members of the merchant marine of the United States."  Any
19  of the following:
20         (1)  Individuals employed as officers or members of crews
21     of vessels documented under the law of the United States or
22     of vessels owned by the United States or of vessels of
23     foreign-flag registry under charter to or control of the
24     United States. This paragraph does not include individuals in
25     military service.
26         (2)  Individuals enrolled with the United States for
27     employment or for training for employment or maintained by
28     the United States for emergency relief service as officers or
29     members of crews of vessels referred to in paragraph (1). The
30     term does not include individuals in military service or
1     individuals employed or enrolled for employment or for
2     training for employment or maintained for emergency relief on
3     the Great Lakes or the inland waterways.
4     "Military elector."  Any of the following:
5         (1)  An individual in military service and the
6     individual's spouse and dependents.
7         (2)  An individual in the merchant marine and the
8     individual's spouse and dependents.
9         (3)  An individual in a religious or welfare group
10     officially attached to and serving with the armed forces of
11     the United States and the individual's spouse and dependents.
12         (4)  An individual who is a civilian employee of the
13     United States outside the territorial limits of the United
14     States, whether or not the individual is subject to the civil
15     service laws and the Classification Act of 1949 (Public Law
16     81-429, 63 Stat. 954) and whether or not the individual is
17     paid from funds appropriated by Congress, and the
18     individual's spouse and dependents.
19     "Municipal election."  The election which the Constitution of
20  Pennsylvania requires to be held in odd-numbered years.
21     "Municipality."  A city, borough, town or township.
22     "November election."  Either the general or the municipal
23  election, or both, according to the context.
24     "Overseas citizen."  An individual who resides outside the
25  territorial limits of the United States and who, but for such
26  residence, would be a qualified elector. The term does not
27  include a military elector.
28     "Party."  Any of the following:
29         (1)  A party or political body, one of whose candidates
30     at the general election immediately preceding the primary:
1             (i)  polled, in each of at least ten counties, at
2         least 2% of the largest entire vote cast in the county
3         for any elected candidate; and
4             (ii)  polled a total vote in this Commonwealth equal
5         to at least 2% of the largest entire vote cast in this
6         Commonwealth for any elected candidate.
7         (2)  A party or political body, one of whose candidates
8     at either the general or municipal election preceding the
9     primary polled at least 5% of the largest entire vote cast
10     for any elected candidate in any county.
11     "Person authorized to administer oaths."  The term includes
12  an individual who is a commissioned officer in military service
13  or a member of the merchant marine of the United States
14  designated for this purpose by the United States Secretary of
15  Commerce.
16     "Person in military service."  A qualified elector who is, by
17  enlistment, enrollment or draft in military service.
18     "Political body."  A political body not recognized as a
19  political party which has filed proper nomination papers as
20  required by law.
21     "Primary election."  An election for the nomination of
22  candidates.
23     "Public office."  Any Federal, State or political subdivision
24  office or position of employment requiring the individual
25  elected or appointed to render public service for a fixed fee or
26  compensation. The term does not include the office of notary
27  public or commissioner of deeds.
28     "Qualified elector."  An individual who possesses all of the
29  qualifications for voting prescribed by the Constitution of
30  Pennsylvania and the laws of this Commonwealth or who, being
1  otherwise qualified by continued residence in the election
2  district, obtains such qualifications before the next ensuing
3  election. The term does not include a military elector.
4     "Registration card."  A registration record containing all
5  information required on the registration application, including
6  the elector's signature, and suitable space for the insertion by
7  the appropriate official of the following information:
8         (1)  The ward and election district of residence.
9         (2)  The registrant's street address.
10         (3)  Data required to be given upon removal from the
11     registrant's residence.
12         (4)  The date of each election at which the registrant
13     votes.
14         (5)  The number and letter of the stub of the ballot
15     issued to the registrant or the registrant's number in the
16     order of admission to the voting machines.
17         (6)  The initials of the election officer who enters the
18     record of voting in the district register.
19         (7)  Whether the registrant needs assistance to vote and,
20     if so, the nature of the disability.
21     "Secretary."  The Secretary of the Commonwealth.
22  Section 103.  Applicability.
23     This act applies to all counties.
24  Section 104.  Construction.
25     No part of any day fixed for the performance of duties by any
26  person or official under this act shall be deemed a Sunday or a
27  legal holiday so as to affect the legality of work done for the
28  purpose of carrying out this act or the right of a person to
29  compensation provided for rendering any service required by this
30  act or so as to relieve any person from doing on such day
1  whatever is necessary for the purpose of carrying out this act.
2  Such services are declared to be necessary public services.
3                             CHAPTER 3
4                        REGISTRATION SYSTEM
5  Section 301.  Regulations.
6     (a)  Regulations.--The secretary shall promulgate regulations
7  necessary to administer this act. The regulations shall include
8  provisions which do all of the following:
9         (1)  Provide for applicants to submit their voter
10     registration application to the commission, the Department of
11     Transportation and other agencies designated in section 525.
12         (2)  Allow the secretary to access the computerized
13     driver's license records of the Department of Transportation
14     for voter registration purposes only.
15         (3)  Provide a system for each county to identify the
16     precinct to which a voter should be assigned for voting
17     purposes.
18         (4)  Prescribe a procedure for the return of completed
19     voter registration applications from the Department of
20     Transportation to the secretary or the appropriate
21     commission.
22     (b)  Advisory committee.--An advisory committee shall be
23  established for the purpose of advising the secretary on the
24  implementation of the provisions of this act. Members of the
25  committee must have a knowledge of election procedures in this
26  Commonwealth.
27         (1)  A ten-member committee shall be formed consisting of
28     the secretary and nine members to be appointed by the
29     Governor. The Governor shall appoint the following:
30             (i)  One person from a county of the first class.
1             (ii)  One person from a county of the second class.
2             (iii)  One person from a county of the second class
3         A.
4             (iv)  Three people from counties of the third, fourth
5         or fifth class.
6             (v)  Three people from counties of the sixth, seventh
7         or eighth class.
8         (2)  The members of the advisory committee shall serve at
9     the Governor's discretion. The committee shall dissolve
10     December 31, 1997.
11  Section 302.  Existing electors.
12     All electors registered to vote in this Commonwealth on the
13  effective date of this act remain registered under this act.
14  Section 303.  Commissions.
15     (a)  Establishment.--A registration commission is established
16  in each county. A commission has jurisdiction over the
17  registration of electors of the county under this act.
18     (b)  Membership.--
19         (1)  Except as provided in paragraphs (2) and (3), the
20     county commissioners of each county shall act as a
21     registration commission for the county.
22         (2)  In a city of the first class, the commission shall
23     consist of the three elected commissioners of the city.
24         (3)  In counties which have adopted home rule charters or
25     optional plans, the commission shall consist of the members
26     of the county body which performs legislative functions
27     unless the county charter or optional plan provides for the
28     appointment of the commission. In either case, there must be
29     minority representation on the commission. The county body
30     which performs legislative functions shall, if the commission
1     does not contain minority representation, appoint such
2     representation from a list submitted by the county chair of
3     the minority party.
4     (c)  Jurisdiction.--A commission has jurisdiction over the
5  registration of electors in the county.
6     (d)  Compensation.--Commissioners shall receive no
7  compensation in addition to their compensation as county or city
8  commissioners.
9     (e)  Majority vote required.--Actions of a commission must be
10  decided by a majority vote of all members except as otherwise
11  provided in this act.
12     (f)  Records.--A commission shall keep a permanent record of
13  its proceedings.
14     (g)  Regulations.--A commission may make regulations, not
15  inconsistent with this act, to do all of the following:
16         (1)  Govern the public sessions of the commission.
17         (2)  Perform the duties imposed by this act.
18         (3)  Carry into effect the provisions of this act and
19     permit the efficient administration of this act, including
20     the designation of duties to be performed and functions to be
21     exercised under this act at its respective offices.
22     (h)  Enforcement.--A commission may enforce regulations,
23  orders and subpoenas as provided in section 1101 and, if
24  necessary, shall have the assistance of the appropriate court of
25  common pleas and of all public officers subservient to the
26  court, in enforcing the regulations, orders and subpoenas.
27     (i)  Errors.--A commission has the power to correct an error
28  or irregularity in registration and to cancel the registration
29  of an individual whom it finds to be improperly registered,
30  subject only to this act. Written notice must be mailed or
1  delivered to each individual whose registration is canceled.
2     (j)  Investigation.--A commission, or a commissioner, has the
3  power to do any of the following:
4         (1)  Summon and interrogate any person concerning the
5     registration of electors or any matter related thereto.
6         (2)  Investigate irregularities in registration.
7         (3)  Summon and examine witnesses.
8         (4)  Require the production of relevant documents.
9     (k)  Oaths and affirmations.--Each commissioner has the power
10  to administer oaths and affirmations. Each individual testifying
11  before a commission or commissioner must be sworn or affirmed.
12     (l)  Correction of errors.--
13         (1)  A commission has the power to correct its records
14     without requiring action of a registered elector in any of
15     the following cases:
16             (i)  If the mailing address of the registered elector
17         has been changed by the renaming of a street, the
18         renumbering of a house or the changing of a post office.
19             (ii)  If the election district of the registered
20         elector has been changed through a change in the
21         boundaries of an election district.
22         (2)  Notice of action under paragraph (1) shall be mailed
23     promptly to the affected registered elector.
24  Section 304.  Commission staff.
25     (a)  Counties of the first class.--
26         (1)  A commissioner may act as registrar or inspector of
27     registration.
28         (2)  The commission has the power to assign assistants,
29     employees or clerks to act as registrars or inspectors of
30     registration. Individuals assigned under this paragraph shall
1     not receive additional compensation for service as registrars
2     or inspectors.
3         (3)  Appointments shall be as follows:
4             (i)  The commission shall annually appoint at least
5         two registrars for each registration place. Not more than
6         one-half of the number of registrars appointed annually
7         for each registration place may be members of the same
8         political party.
9             (ii)  Registrars appointed must be qualified electors
10         of the election district or ward for which they have been
11         appointed, must be of good moral character, must not have
12         been convicted of a crime and must be familiar with the
13         qualifications of electors and the duties of the
14         registrars. No individual who holds or is a candidate for
15         public or party office may be appointed to or hold any
16         office or employment under any commission, except as
17         otherwise provided by this act.
18             (iii)  Appointments shall be as follows:
19                 (A)  The commission shall provide 15 days' notice
20             for submission of candidates.
21                 (B)  The city chairs of the party having polled
22             the highest and the second highest vote in this
23             Commonwealth at the last preceding gubernatorial
24             election may file with the commission a written list
25             of names of members of the party whom the chair
26             recommends for appointment as registrars. Lists must
27             contain the name, address, qualifications and
28             occupation of each individual recommended and shall
29             be open to public inspection in the office of the
30             commission.
1                 (C)  The commission shall appoint individuals
2             from the list as registrars representing the parties.
3             If more candidates are recommended by a chair than
4             the party is entitled to have appointed, the
5             commission shall appoint those candidates from the
6             list whom the commission finds to be qualified under
7             this section. If there are not sufficient candidates
8             qualified to serve recommended by a chair, the
9             commission may appoint other individuals whom the
10             commission believes qualified.
11                 (D)  If a vacancy occurs in the office of
12             registrar, the commission shall fill the vacancy by
13             appointing an elector of the district or ward, as the
14             case may be, who is qualified under subparagraph (ii)
15             and who is a registered and enrolled member of the
16             same political party as the registrar whose office
17             was vacated. The vacancy appointment shall be made in
18             the same manner as the initial appointment.
19             (iv)  Registrars appointed have the power to register
20         the qualified electors of the election district or ward
21         and, in so doing, to administer oaths and affirmations.
22         Registrars appointed shall perform all other duties
23         imposed on registrars by this act and by the commission.
24             (v)  Nothing in this subsection shall prevent a
25         registrar from serving as a registrar in a ward or
26         division of the city or prevent the commission from
27         assigning a registrar to register voters in a ward or
28         division of the city.
29             (vi)  Except as provided in paragraph (1), each
30         registrar shall receive compensation set by the
1         commission for each day during which the registrar is
2         engaged in the active performance of duties as registrar.
3             (vii)  The commission shall designate the duties to
4         be performed by appointed registrars.
5         (4)  Any insertion or removal of names or other
6     information in registers or an amendment of records done by
7     any employee, registrar or inspector by order of the
8     commission shall be construed to be an action of the
9     commission. It is the duty of the commission to correct any
10     error in such action.
11         (5)  Before exercising a power of office, a registrar or
12     inspector of registration assigned under paragraph (2) or
13     appointed under paragraph (3) must comply with all of the
14     following:
15             (i)  Take an oath or affirmation of office, as
16         prescribed by the commission.
17             (ii)  Receive from the commission a certificate of
18         appointment setting forth name and address, date of
19         appointment and the length of appointment.
20         (6)  The commission may annually designate registration
21     places in the wards.
22         (7)  The commission may appoint employees necessary to
23     perform the functions of the office and to register the
24     voters of the city. Employees shall be paid compensation as
25     set by the commission.
26         (8)  Removal shall be as follows:
27             (i)  The commission has the power to remove any
28         employee, inspector, registrar or other officer appointed
29         or employed by it. No registrar appointed under paragraph
30         (3) may be removed except for cause.
1             (ii)  A qualified elector of the city may appear
2         before the commission and show that a registrar assigned
3         under paragraph (2) or appointed under paragraph (3) does
4         not possess the qualifications requisite for the
5         performance of duties of office or has violated this act.
6         If, after public hearing, the commission finds the
7         charges brought to be true, the commission shall remove
8         the registrar.
9         (9)  An inspector of registration has the authority to
10     investigate all matters regarding voter registration and to
11     make recommendations to the commission.
12         (10)  The commission may employ counsel. Counsel shall
13     advise the commission regarding its powers and duties, the
14     rights of electors and the best methods of legal procedure
15     for carrying out this act and shall appear for and represent
16     the commission on all appeals under Chapter 13. The amount of
17     compensation for counsel shall be set by the city
18     commissioners.
19         (11)  Employees and clerks of the commission shall be
20     privileged from arrest while acting as registrars.
21     Commissioners and inspectors of registration shall be
22     privileged from arrest while performing their duties, except
23     upon warrant of a court of record for a felony, for wanton
24     breach of the peace or for a criminal violation of this act.
25     (b)  Counties of the second through eighth classes.--
26         (1)  The commission shall appoint a chief clerk, who may
27     be the chief clerk of the county commissioners. The chief
28     clerk has authority to administer oaths and to sign vouchers.
29         (2)  Registrars and inspectors of registration must be
30     qualified electors of the county. Registrars and inspectors
1     of registration shall receive compensation, either on a per
2     diem basis for time actually employed or on the basis of work
3     actually done, as fixed by the salary board of the county. No
4     registrar or inspector of registration may exercise any power
5     of office until taking an oath or affirmation of office
6     prescribed by the commission and receiving from the
7     commission a certificate of appointment setting forth name
8     and address, date of appointment and length of appointment.
9         (3)  Other staff shall be as follows:
10             (i)  The commission may appoint assistants and
11         employees as necessary to carry out this act and may
12         remove them. The number and compensation of assistants
13         and employees shall be fixed by the salary board of the
14         county. A commission may appoint employees of the county
15         to act for the commission without additional
16         compensation.
17             (ii)  The employees to be appointed by the commission
18         include:
19                 (A)  Registrars or clerks who are empowered to
20             register the qualified electors of the county and, in
21             so doing, to administer oaths and affirmations.
22                 (B)  Inspectors of registration who have
23             authority to investigate all matters regarding voter
24             registration and to make recommendations to the
25             commission.
26         (4)  The county solicitor shall be counsel for the
27     commission and shall receive no compensation in addition to
28     compensation as county solicitor. Counsel shall advise the
29     commission regarding its powers and duties and the rights of
30     electors and concerning the best methods of legal procedure
1     for carrying out this act and shall appear for and represent
2     the commission on appeals taken from its decisions or orders.
3         (5)  Any insertion or removal of names or other
4     information in registers and the amendment of records done by
5     any employee of the commission or by order of the commission
6     shall be construed to have been done by the commission. The
7     commission shall be responsible for the correction of errors.
8         (6)  No individual who holds or is a candidate for public
9     or party office may be appointed to or hold any office or
10     employment under any commission, except as otherwise provided
11     by this act.
12         (7)  Commissioners, registrars and inspectors of
13     registration shall be privileged from arrest while performing
14     their duties except upon warrant of a court of record for a
15     felony, for wanton breach of the peace or for a criminal
16     violation of this act.
17         (8)  A commissioner or the chief clerk may act as
18     registrar or inspector of registration. When acting under
19     this paragraph, the commissioner or chief clerk has the
20     powers and duties conferred by law upon registrars and
21     inspectors of registration.
22  Section 305.  Police assistance.
23     A commission may call on police officers of the appropriate
24  municipality to maintain the peace at a place of registration.

30     (c)  Source of funds.--A commission may accept and use in the

22  for commercial or improper purposes.

26                           QUALIFICATIONS
27  Section 501.  Qualifications to register.
28     (a)  Eligibility.--A qualified elector who will be at least
29  18 years of age on the day of the next election, who has been a
30  citizen of the United States for at least one month and who has
1  resided in this Commonwealth and the election district where the
2  qualified elector offers to vote for at least 30 days prior to
3  the next ensuing election shall be entitled to be registered as
4  provided in this chapter. If an individual is qualified to vote
5  in an election district prior to removal of residence, the
6  individual may, if a resident of this Commonwealth, vote in the
7  election district from which residence was removed within the 30
8  days preceding the election.
9     (b)  Effect.--No individual shall be permitted to vote at any
10  election unless the individual is registered under this
11  subsection except as provided by law or by order of a court of
12  common pleas. No registered elector shall be required to
13  register again for any election while the elector continues to
14  reside at the same address UNLESS THE REGISTERED ELECTOR'S        <--
15  REGISTRATION IS CANCELED BY REASON OF FAILURE TO VOTE FOR THE
16  PERIOD OF YEARS PROVIDED IN SECTION 911.
17  Section 502.  Residence of electors.
18     (a)  General rule.--
19         (1)  For the purpose of registration and voting, no
20     individual shall be deemed to have gained a residence by
21     reason of presence or lost a residence by reason of absence
22     in any of the following circumstances:
23             (i)  Being employed in the service, either civil or
24         military, of this Commonwealth or of the United States.
25             (ii)  Being engaged in the navigation of the waters
26         of this Commonwealth or of the United States or on the
27         high seas.
28             (iii)  Being in an institution at public expense.
29         This subparagraph does not apply to a veteran who resides
30         in a home for disabled and indigent soldiers and sailors
1         maintained by the Commonwealth. Such a veteran may elect
2         to utilize that residence for registration and voting or
3         elect to vote as an absentee elector by the use of an
4         absentee ballot.
5         (2)  Nothing in paragraph (1) shall preclude any elector
6     eligible under section 501 from establishing the district of
7     residence as the election district of residence pursuant to
8     subsection (b).
9         (3)  Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, no
10     individual who is confined in a penal institution shall be
11     deemed a resident of the election district where the
12     institution is located. The individual shall be deemed to
13     reside where the individual was last registered before being
14     confined in the penal institution; or, if there was no
15     registration prior to confinement, the individual shall be
16     deemed to reside at the last known address before
17     confinement.
18         (4)  An individual who resides at an institution for the
19     mentally ill or the mentally retarded, if otherwise qualified
20     under section 501, shall be deemed, at the individual's
21     option, a resident in one of the following:
22             (i)  The district where the institution is located.
23             (ii)  The district where the individual was last
24         registered to vote before entering the institution. For
25         purposes of this subparagraph, if the individual was not
26         registered before entering the institution, the
27         individual shall be deemed to reside at the last known
28         address before entering the institution.
29     (b)  Rules for determination.--The following apply:
30         (1)  That place shall be considered the residence of an
1     individual in which habitation is fixed and to which,
2     whenever the individual is absent, the individual has the
3     intention of returning.
4         (2)  An individual shall not be considered to have lost
5     residence if the individual leaves home and goes into another
6     state or another election district for temporary purposes
7     only, with the intention of returning.
8         (3)  An individual shall not be considered to have gained
9     a residence in an election district if the individual comes
10     into that district for temporary purposes only, without the
11     intention of making that election district a permanent place
12     of abode.
13         (4)  If an individual removes to another state with the
14     intention of making that state the permanent residence, the
15     individual shall be considered to have lost residence in this
16     Commonwealth.
17         (5)  If an individual removes to another state with the
18     intention of remaining there an indefinite time and making
19     that state the place of residence, the individual shall be
20     considered to have lost residence in this Commonwealth,
21     notwithstanding an intention to return at some indefinite
22     future period.
23         (6)  If an individual goes into another state and, while
24     there, votes in an election held by that state, the
25     individual shall be considered to have lost residence in this
26     Commonwealth.
27         (7)  An individual employed in the service of the Federal
28     Government or of the Commonwealth and required thereby to be
29     absent from the municipality where the individual resided
30     when entering that employment, and the spouse of the
1     individual, may remain registered as of the district where
2     the individual resided immediately prior to entering that
3     employment; and the individual and the spouse shall be
4     enrolled in the political party designated by the individual
5     or spouse without declaring a residence by street and number.
6             (i)  An individual who registers under this paragraph
7         for Commonwealth employment must produce a certificate
8         from the head of the State agency, under the seal of
9         office, setting forth that the individual or the
10         individual's spouse is actually employed in the service
11         of the Commonwealth and setting forth the nature of the
12         employment and the time when the employee first entered
13         the employment. The commission shall retain certificates
14         under this subparagraph.
15             (ii)  The commission shall note on the registration
16         card of each individual registered under this paragraph
17         the fact of Federal or State employment.
18             (iii)  At least once every two years the commission
19         shall verify the employment of the individuals registered
20         under this paragraph at the proper Federal or State
21         office. If an individual is found to be no longer a
22         Federal or State employee, the individual's registration
23         shall be canceled under Chapter 9.

26  Section 521.  Methods of voter registration.
27     An individual qualified to register to vote under section
28  501(a) may apply to register as follows:
29         (1)  In person under section 522.
30         (2)  In conjunction with an application for a motor
1     vehicle driver's license under section 523.
2         (3)  By mail application under section 524.
3         (4)  In conjunction with certain government agency

5  Section 522.  In-person voter registration.
6     (a)  General rule.--Applications may be submitted to register
7  to vote or change party enrollment or name or address on current
8  registration record in person before the registration commission
9  or a commissioner, a registrar or a clerk at the office of the
10  commission or at a place designated by the commission. The
11  applicant shall be advised that any intentional false statement
12  on the application constitutes perjury and will be punishable as
13  such. The applicant shall provide the information required on
14  the registration application and sign the registration
15  declaration. The commission shall prepare and provide voter
16  registration applications for the purpose of registering
17  qualified electors in accordance with this section.
18  Notwithstanding any other provision of this act, the commission
19  may use a mail registration application for in-person
20  registration.
21     (b)  Notice.--The commission shall, within a reasonable time,
22  publicly announce the address of each place of registration, the
23  address of each office of the commission established for the
24  registration of electors other than it's main office and the
25  days and hours when the place or office is open for the
26  registration of electors. The announcement shall be made by
27  posting notice at the place or office and at the commission's
28  main office and by other means as the commission deems
29  advisable.
30     (c)  Polls.--The election board of each county shall cause
1  any polling place to be open, in proper order for use, as a
2  place of registration, on each day when the polling place is
3  desired by the commission or required by the provisions of this
4  act for use as a place of registration. The appropriating
5  authority of the county shall provide for the payment of rentals
6  for such polling places and other places of registration.
7     (d)  Schools.--The board of public education or the board of
8  school directors shall furnish suitable space in any public
9  school building under its jurisdiction or control and shall
10  cause the space to be open and in proper order for use as a
11  place of registration on each day when the space is desired by
12  the commission for use as a place of registration in accordance
13  with the provisions of this act. Use under this subsection may
14  not interfere with school instruction.
15     (e)  Municipal buildings.--The proper authority in the county
16  or municipality shall furnish suitable space in a building under
17  its jurisdiction or control and shall cause the space to be open
18  and in proper order for use as a place of registration on each
19  day when the space is desired by the commission for use as a
20  place of registration. Use under this subsection may not
21  interfere with the use for which the space is primarily
22  designed.

5  section 527, the application shall be retained by the commission
6  until the beginning of the next period during which registration
7  can be made.

9         (1)  A military elector may apply at any time for
10     registration on an official registration application or any
11     form prescribed by the Federal Government for such purpose.

22     not left that home address but continued to reside there.
23         (3)  The commission is authorized to consider a request
24     for an absentee ballot as a request for an official
25     registration application and to forward to the requester all
26     of the following:
27             (i)  An absentee ballot and balloting material.
28             (ii)  An official registration application.

5  Section 526.  Time.
6     (a)  General rule.--Except as provided in subsection (b),
7  each commission, commissioners and registrars or clerks
8  appointed by the commission shall receive, during ordinary
9  business hours and during additional hours as the commission
10  prescribes, at the office of the commission and at additional
11  places as the commission designates, applications from
12  individuals who appear and claim that they are entitled to be
13  registered as electors of a municipality.
14     (b)  Deadlines.--In the administration of voter registration,
15  each commission shall ensure that any eligible applicant is
16  registered to vote in an election when the applicant has met any
17  of the following conditions:
18         (1)  In the case of voter registration with a motor
19     vehicle driver's license application under section 523, if
20     the valid voter registration application is submitted to the
21     appropriate Department of Transportation office not later
22     than 30 days before the date of the election.
23         (2)  In the case of registration by mail under section
24     524, if the valid voter registration mail application is
25     postmarked not later than the deadline to register for the
26     ensuing election or, in the case of an illegible or missing
27     postmark, is received not later than five days after the
28     deadline to register for the ensuing election.
29         (3)  In the case of voter registration at a voter
30     registration agency under section 525, if the valid voter
1     registration application is accepted at the voter
2     registration agency not later than 30 days before the date of
3     the election.
4         (4)  In any other case, if the valid voter registration
5     application of the applicant is received by the appropriate
6     commission not later than 30 days before the election.
7     (c)  Special rules.--
8         (1)  In the case of a special election within a
9     congressional, senatorial or representative district held on
10     a day other than the day of a primary, general or municipal
11     election, the registration application forms shall not be
12     processed in the wards and election districts comprising the
13     district for the 30 days prior to the special election for
14     such election.
15         (2)  No applications shall be received as follows:
16             (i)  On Sundays.
17             (ii)  On holidays.
18             (iii)  On the day of the election.
19             (iv)  During the 30 days next preceding each
20         general, municipal and primary election except as
21         provided in subsection (b).

23  Section 528.  Approval of registration applications.
24     (a)  Examination.--
25         (1)  Official registration applications submitted to a
26     registration commission shall be examined by the commissioner
27     or a clerk or registrar upon receipt.
28         (2)  If the official voter registration application
29     submission is in person, the application shall be attested
30     and dated by the commissioner, clerk or registrar.
1         (3)  Upon receipt of any other voter registration
2     application, the commission shall make an entry of the date
3     received on the application. If the applicant does not reside
4     within the commission's county but resides elsewhere in this
5     Commonwealth, the commission shall forward the application
6     card to the proper commission and make a record of the
7     forwarding.
8     (b)  Decision.--
9         (1)  If the commission finds the official registration
10     application not properly completed, the application shall be
11     rejected. The commission shall make reasonable efforts to
12     complete the registration before rejecting it for omissions
13     and inconsistencies. If the commission rejects an application
14     under this paragraph, it shall indicate "REJECTED" on the
15     application and state there the reason for rejection and
16     notify the applicant by first class nonforwardable mail,
17     return postage guaranteed.
18         (2)  If the official registration application contains
19     the required information indicating that the applicant is
20     legally qualified to register as stated in the application,
21     the commission shall mail to the applicant a voter's
22     identification card in accordance with subsection (d)(1).
23         (3)  For an application for a transfer of registration or
24     a change in address or name, if the application contains the
25     required information and the applicant is legally qualified
26     to transfer registration or change name or address as stated
27     in the application, the commission shall make the transfer or
28     change. If the commission suspects that the applicant is not
29     entitled to transfer registration or to change name or
30     address, the commission may investigate. If the commission
1     finds that the applicant is not entitled to the transfer or
2     change, the application shall be rejected. The applicant
3     shall be notified of the rejection and the reason for it.
4     Rejection must be made no later than ten days before the
5     election succeeding the filing of the application.
6     (c)  Result.--If an application is designed to serve as a
7  registration card, the accepted application of an elector may
8  serve as the elector's official registration card if it was so
9  designed and shall be filed in the office of the commission in
10  accordance with Chapter 7. If the application is not designed to
11  serve as a registration card, the information contained on the
12  application, including the elector's signature, shall be
13  transferred to the registration card. If a commission utilizes a
14  signature digitization list, it shall transfer all information
15  contained on the application, including the signature, to a
16  computer file. The right of an individual to be registered shall
17  not be subject to challenge for any reason other than failure to
18  have furnished the commission a properly completed registration
19  application. Applicants for registration shall be challenged
20  under section 529.
21     (d)  Disposition.--
22         (1)  When the registration of an elector has been finally
23     processed under subsection (b)(2), the commission shall
24     transmit to the applicant by first-class nonforwardable mail
25     a wallet-sized voter's identification card, which shall serve
26     as notice to the applicant of the disposition of the
27     application. The card shall contain all of the following:
28             (i)  Name and address of the elector.
29             (ii)  Name of municipality.
30             (iii)  Identification of elector's ward and district.
1             (iv)  Effective date of registration.
2             (v)  Designation of party enrollment and date of
3         enrollment.
4             (vi)  A space for the elector's signature or mark.
5             (vii)  A statement that the card relates only to the
6         time of issuance of the card and is not of itself
7         evidence or proof of the qualifications of the elector to
8         vote at an election and that it is not necessary to
9         present the card when voting. The statements required by
10         this subparagraph shall be placed on the reverse side of
11         the card from where all of the other information required
12         by this paragraph is placed.
13         (2)  The carrier envelope in which the identification
14     card is enclosed shall contain on the outside a request to
15     the postmaster to return it within five days if it cannot be
16     delivered to the addressee at the address given.
17         (3)  No registration application shall be deemed to be
18     accepted until ten days after the voter's identification card

16  Section 529.  Challenges.
17     (a)  Standing.--An individual claiming the right to register
18  may be challenged by a commissioner, registrar or clerk or by a
19  qualified elector of the municipality.
20     (b)  Complaint.--To make a challenge, a complainant must file
21  a challenge affidavit in a form prescribed by the secretary
22  containing the following information:
23         (1)  Name of challenged applicant.
24         (2)  Address of challenged applicant.
25         (3)  Name of complainant.
26         (4)  Address of complainant.
27         (5)  Date of affidavit.
28         (6)  Reason for challenge.
29     (c)  Response.--An individual who is challenged must respond
30  to the challenge affidavit as set forth in subsection (b) in a
1  written statement sworn or affirmed by the individual. The
2  challenged applicant must produce such other evidence as may be
3  required to satisfy the registrar or commissioner as to the
4  applicant's qualifications as an elector.
5     (d)  Resolution.--If the challenged applicant establishes, to
6  the satisfaction of the commission, the right to be registered
7  as required by this act, the challenged applicant shall be
8  registered.
9  Section 530.  Appeals.
10     (a)  Right.--An applicant whose application to be registered
11  has been denied may file with the commission a petition to be
12  registered, setting forth the grounds of the petition under oath
13  or affirmation. The petition must be filed by the 15th day prior
14  to an election.
15     (b)  Hearing.--
16         (1)  The commission shall fix a time for a public hearing
17     at its office not later than the tenth day prior to the
18     election.
19         (2)  The commission shall give the person responsible for
20     the rejection 48 hours' notice of the hearing.
21         (3)  At the hearing, a clerk, inspector of registration
22     or qualified elector of the county may offer evidence as to
23     why the petitioner should not be registered.
24         (4)  The commission, if satisfied that the petitioner is
25     entitled to be registered, shall direct registration.

28  Section 701.  General register.
29     (a)  Original registration cards.--If a commission uses the
30  original registration cards for registered electors in the
1  county as the district register, the duplicate registration
2  cards or photocopies of the original registration cards shall be
3  placed in exact alphabetical order by last name of the
4  registrant, shall be indexed and shall be kept at the office of
5  the registration commission in a manner as to be properly
6  safeguarded. These cards constitute the general register of the
7  county. They may not be removed from the office of the
8  commission except upon order of a court of record. Nothing in
9  this act shall preclude the use of duplicate registration cards
10  from applications provided under the act of March 30, 1937
11  (P.L.115, No.40), known as The First Class City Permanent
12  Registration Act, or the act of April 29, 1937 (P.L.487,
13  No.115), known as The Permanent Registration Act for Cities of
14  the Second Class, Cities of the Second Class A, Cities of the
15  Third Class, Boroughs, Towns, and Townships.
16     (b)  Digitized signature lists.-- If a commission uses
17  digitized signature lists as the district register, the original
18  registration cards shall be placed in exact alphabetical order
19  by last name of the registrant, indexed and kept at the office
20  of the commission in a manner as to be properly safeguarded.
21  These original cards constitute the general register of the
22  county. They may not be removed from the office of the
23  commission except upon order of a court of record. The
24  commission shall safely retain all registration cards used in
25  the registration of electors or in conducting an election. If a
26  commission has the capability to accept an electronic
27  application, the secretary may require the commission to produce
28  a computer-generated card to file in the general register. If
29  the commission finds a record for a registrant on the computer
30  data base which is not contained in the general register, it
1  shall replace the original card with a computer-generated
2  duplicate card upon written permission from the registrant or
3  upon order of a court of record.
4     (c)  Duplicate files.--If the commission deems a duplicate
5  file of registration cards necessary for administrative
6  purposes, the commission may prepare a reproduction in
7  compliance with the following:
8         (1)  The registration form shall be photographed,
9     microphotographed or reproduced in a manner approved for
10     permanent records by the secretary.
11         (2)  The device used to reproduce the card shall be one
12     which accurately reproduces the original in all details.
13         (3)  The photographs, microphotographs or other
14     reproductions shall be open to public inspection and
15     provision is made for preserving, examining and using them.
16  Section 702.  District registers.
17     (a)  Registration card file.--
18         (1)  Except as provided in subsection (b) and in section
19     701(b), the original registration cards shall be filed by
20     election districts in exact alphabetical order by last name
21     of the registrant and shall be indexed.
22         (2)  The cards constitute the district register.
23         (3)  The commission shall provide binders, which are
24     capable of being locked, for filing and indexing the
25     registration cards. The keys to the binders shall at all
26     times be retained by the commission.
27         (4)  The district register shall be kept at the office of
28     the commission except as provided in subsection (b) and shall
29     be open to public inspection, subject to reasonable
30     safeguards and regulations.
1     (b)  Computer lists.--
2         (1)  Instead of using registration cards as the district
3  register as provided in subsection (a), a commission may, at its
4  option and at county expense, use a computer list or computer-
5  generated cards containing the registered electors arranged by
6  election district, alphabetically by last name of the
7  registrant.
8         (2)  The computer list or computer-generated cards shall
9     be in a form prescribed by the secretary and shall contain
10     necessary information that would otherwise be available on
11     the registration cards, including a legible digitized
12     signature of the registrant copied from the signature on the
13     registration card. The district election officials shall have
14     computer printouts at the polling places containing the
15     necessary information needed to verify the identity of the
16     elector. The digitized signature list shall be open to public
17     inspection, subject to reasonable safeguards and regulations.
18         (3)  A commission may, during systems conversion periods
19     or emergency conditions, provide for a district register
20     containing the original registration cards. The following
21     apply:
22             (i)  The original registration cards shall be filed
23         by election district in one of the following orders as
24         determined by the commission:
25                 (A)  Exact alphabetical order by last name of
26             registrant.
27                 (B)  Order in which registrants' residences
28             appear upon the streets of the election district, in
29             exact alphabetical order by last name of registrant
30             for each residence.
1             (ii)  The district register containing registration
2         cards shall be kept at the office of the commission and
3         shall be open for public inspection, subject to
4         reasonable safeguards and regulations and to the
5         provisions of this act.
6     (c)  Currency.--It is the duty of the commission to compare
7  and correct the general register and district registers to
8  ensure their accuracy. By noon of the third day preceding an
9  election, the commission shall have the district register for
10  each election district accurately corrected to date.
11     (d)  Delivery.--The commission shall deliver, in the manner
12  required by law for delivery of election materials, the district
13  register to the election officers for use on election day.
14     (e)  Form.--District registers shall be enclosed within a
15  case or container and shall be locked and sealed by the
16  commission before delivery. The district register shall have
17  printed or written thereon the words "District Register of
18  Voters" and the district and ward, if any.
19     (f)  Examination.--Immediately following each election, the
20  commission shall cause each district register to be examined.
21  The commission specifically shall compare the signature of each
22  elector on each voter's certificate with the elector's signature
23  in the district register and shall report in writing to the
24  district attorney any evidence or indication of probable fraud,
25  impersonation or forgery which may appear to the commission by
26  reason of the comparison. In the case of any elector whom the
27  election officers have recorded as removed, deceased or
28  challenged and have prevented from voting, the commission shall
29  ascertain the facts and shall correct the general and district
30  registers in accordance with Chapter 9.
1  Section 703.  Street lists.
2     (a)  Preparation.--Commencing not later than the 15th day
3  prior to each election, the commission shall prepare for each
4  election district a list of the names and addresses of all
5  registered electors as of that date resident in the district.
6  The list may not include the digitized signature of a registered
7  voter. The list shall be arranged in one of the following
8  manners:
9         (1)  By streets and house numbers.
10         (2)  Alphabetically by last name of registrant.
11         (3)  In a manner whereby the location of the elector's
12     residence can be identified.
13     (b)  Retention.--The commission shall retain two copies of
14  the list under subsection (a) on file at its office. These
15  copies shall be available for public inspection during business
16  hours, subject to reasonable safeguards and regulations.
17     (c)  Distribution.--The commission shall distribute the list
18  under subsection (a) upon request as follows:
19         (1)  To officials concerned with the conduct of
20     elections.
21         (2)  To political parties and political bodies.
22         (3)  To candidates.
23         (4)  To organized bodies of citizens, such as community
24     service organizations and the League of Women Voters.
25  Section 704.  Public information lists.
26     (a)  Establishment.--
27         (1)  The commission may provide for computer inquiries
28  concerning individual registered voters. With respect to each
29  voter who is the subject of an inquiry, the information provided
30  shall contain the name, address, year of birth and voting
1  history. Upon request, the commission shall supply a printed
2  record for each such voter subject to the provisions of this
3  act. In addition, the commission may make available for
4  inspection a printed or computerized public information list
5  containing the name, address, year of birth and voting history
6  of each registered voter in the county.
7         (2)  The list may also include information on voting
8     districts.
9         (3)  The list may not contain the digitized signature of
10     the elector.
11     (b)  Access.--
12         (1)  The commission may promulgate reasonable regulations
13     governing access to the list.
14         (2)  No individual inspecting the list may tamper with or
15     alter it.
16         (3)  No individual who inspects the list or who acquires
17     names of registered voters from the list may use information
18     contained in the list for purposes unrelated to elections,
19     political activities or law enforcement. Before inspecting
20     the list or obtaining names of voters or other information
21     from the list, the individual must provide identification to
22     the public official having custody of the public information
23     list and must state in writing that any information obtained
24     from the list will not be used for purposes unrelated to
25     elections, political activities or law enforcement.
26         (4)  The secretary may provide copies of public
27     information lists for uses related to elections or political
28     activities or in response to a law enforcement inquiry from a
29     public official concerning a failure to comply with any
30     criminal statute or any tax statute or ordinance. Requests to
1     examine or obtain information from the public information
2     lists must be made and processed in the manner provided in
3     the regulations of the secretary. Upon receipt of a copy of
4     the court order, the secretary shall withhold from the public
5     information list the name of any registered voter placed

7     (c)  Copies.--
8         (1)  The commission shall provide paper copies of the
9     public information lists and may provide copies in some other

13     shall be determined by the office providing copies.
14         (2)  An individual who inspects or acquires a copy of a
15     public information list may not use any information contained
16     in it for purposes unrelated to elections, political
17     activities or law enforcement.

22  Section 901.  Removal notices.
23     (a)  Form.--
24         (1)  The commission shall make removal notices available
25     to electors who are registered in the county.
26         (2)  The notice shall be printed upon cards suitable for
27     mailing, addressed to the office of the commission. The
28     notice shall provide the following information:
29             (i)  The address of present residence, including
30         municipality.
1             (ii)  The address of last registration, including
2         municipality.
3             (iii)  Date of removal to present residence.
4             (iv)  Signature.
5         (3)  The notice shall contain a statement that the
6     elector may, by filling out properly and signing a removal
7     notice and returning it to the office of the commission,
8     secure the transfer of registration, effective as to
9     elections at least 30 days after the date of removal into the
10     new district.
11         (4)  The notice shall contain a warning to the elector
12     that the notice will not be accepted as an application for
13     transfer of the elector's registration unless the signature
14     thereon can be identified by the commission as the elector's
15     signature as it appears on file with the commission.
16         (5)  The notice shall contain a warning to the elector
17     that the notice must be received by the commission not later
18     than 30 days before the election. If mailed, the notice must
19     be postmarked not later than the deadline for registration
20     or, in the case of an illegible or missing postmark, received
21     within five days of the close of registration.
22     (b)  Use.--An elector who removes residence from one place to
23  another within the same county must notify the commission by
24  filing a removal notice under subsection (a), or a signed
25  request for renewal that contains the information required in
26  subsection (a), with the commission not later than the
27  registration deadline before the election. If mailed, the notice
28  or request must be postmarked not later than the deadline for
29  registration or, in the case of an illegible or missing
30  postmark, received within five days of the close of
1  registration. The following apply:
2         (1)  An official registration application of an elector
3     who has registered by mail qualifies as a removal notice.
4         (2)  An elector who removes residence from one place to
5     another within the same county and who has not yet filed a
6     removal notice with the commission shall be permitted to vote
7     at the election next following removal if, at the time of
8     signing voter's certificate, the elector files with the judge
9     of election a signed removal notice properly filled out.
10     Removal notices under this paragraph shall be returned to the
11     commission with the voting check list, and the commission
12     shall proceed to transfer the registration of the electors
13     under section 902.
14  Section 902.  Transfer of registration.
15     (a)  General rule.--Upon timely receipt of notification of
16  removal under section 901(b), the registration commission shall
17  proceed as follows:
18         (1)  The signature on the notification document shall be
19     compared with the signature of the elector as it appears on
20     file with the commission.
21         (2)  If the signature appears authentic, the commission
22     shall enter the change of residence on the registration card
23     of the elector in the general register and district register
24     and shall transfer the registration card of the elector from
25     the district register of the election district of previous
26     residence to the district register of the election district
27     of new residence.
28         (3)  If a request for transfer which is determined to be
29     authentic under paragraph (2) shows a removal within the
30     period of 30 days preceding an election, the commission,
1     after such election, shall enter the change of residence on
2     the registration card of the elector in the general register
3     and district register and shall transfer the registration
4     card of the elector from the district register of the
5     election district of previous residence. The commission shall
6     advise the elector promptly in writing of its action.
7     (b)  Electors unable to write.--An elector who is unable to
8  sign the notification document may affix a mark to the
9  notification document. The mark must be affixed in the presence
10  of a witness, who must sign the notification document.
11  Section 903.  Change of enrollment of political party.
12     By the deadline for registration, a registered voter who
13  desires to change the enrollment of political designation or
14  who, although registered, has not previously enrolled as a
15  member of a party may appear before a commissioner, registrar or
16  clerk, or may submit an application by mail under section 524,
17  and state in a signed writing the political party in which the
18  voter desires to be enrolled. If the signature of the elector is
19  verified by comparison with the elector's signature as it
20  appears on file with the commission, the commissioner, registrar
21  or clerk shall make the change in the general register and
22  district register. If supported by other evidence of identity, a
23  mark may be made in lieu of a signature by an elector who is
24  unable to write. The mark must be made in the presence of a
25  witness, who must sign the registration application.
26  Section 904.  Physical disability.
27     (a)  Eligibility.--If an elector is unable to see or mark the
28  ballot or operate the voting machine or to enter the voting
29  compartment or voting machine booth without assistance, the
30  following shall apply:
1         (1)  The elector may, at least ten days prior to the next
2     election, personally make application to the commission or a
3     registrar or a clerk.
4         (2)  The application must request the entry of the exact
5     nature of the disability on the elector's registration card.
6         (3)  The commission shall make the entry as appropriate.
7         (4)  If the disability is not entered on the registration
8     card, the elector may receive assistance if the elector
9     completes a declaration in the polling place under section
10     1218 of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known as
11     the Pennsylvania Election Code.
12     (b)  Termination.--If the commission ascertains that an
13  elector who has declared need for assistance is no longer in
14  need of assistance, it shall cancel on the registration card the
15  entry relating to illiteracy or physical disability which
16  authorized assistance. The commission shall notify the elector
17  by mail of its action.
18  Section 905.  Department of Health.
19     The commission shall cancel the registration of a registered
20  elector reported dead by the Department of Health. The
21  Department of Health shall, within 45 days of receiving notice
22  of the death of an individual 18 years of age or older, provide
23  the name and address of that individual to the commission on a
24  form prescribed by the department. An individual incorrectly
25  reported deceased by the Department of Health may appear in
26  person before a commissioner, registrar or clerk, at the office
27  of the commission, and prove identity. The commission, upon such
28  proof, shall correct its records.
29  Section 906.  Residence monitoring.
30     (a)  Municipalities.--Subject to the provisions of this act,
1  municipal officers and agencies and public utility corporations
2  furnishing electricity, gas, water or steam to householders in a
3  municipality shall report in writing to the commission, upon the
4  request of the commission and upon forms supplied by the
5  commission, all cases of discontinuance of service to
6  residences. The report shall include, if known, the name of the
7  person who contracted for service and the addresses to which
8  that person has removed.
9     (b)  Real estate brokers and rental agents.--Subject to the
10  provisions of this act, real estate brokers and rental agents
11  shall report in writing to the commission, upon the request of
12  the commission, all cases of residence property managed by them
13  which have been vacated by the tenant. The report shall include,
14  if known, the name of the tenant and the address to which the
15  tenant has removed.
16  Section 907.  Checkup of registers.
17     (a)  General rule.--At any time prior to the 30th day
18  preceding an election, the commission may mail to any qualified
19  elector whose name appears in any district register a notice
20  setting forth the elector's name and address as it appears in
21  the register, requesting the elector in case of any error to
22  present the notice within ten days at the office of the
23  commission to have the error corrected and warning that any
24  discrepancy between the qualified elector's actual name and
25  address and the name and address as recorded in the original
26  register will constitute ground for challenging the elector's
27  vote. The notice shall contain on the outside, "Do not forward,
28  return to Board of Elections," and a request of the Postal
29  Service to return it within five days if it cannot be delivered
30  to the addressee at the address given.
1     (b)  Checkup by Postal Service.--At any time prior to the
2  30th day preceding an election, the commission may cause a
3  checkup to be made by the Postal Service of any qualified
4  elector whose name appears in any district register.
5     (c)  Quadrennial checkups.--At least once in each four years
6  the commission may conduct a checkup of each registered elector
7  by either of the methods provided for in subsections (a) and
8  (b).
9     (d)  Failure to deliver notice.--Upon the return by the
10  Postal Service of any notice which it has been unable to deliver
11  at the given address because the addressee cannot be found there
12  or upon report by the Postal Service that any registered elector
13  does not reside at the address given on the registration card,
14  the commission shall do one of the following:
15         (1)  Direct an authorized employee to visit in person the
16     address of the qualified elector and, if the employee finds
17     that the qualified elector does not reside at the address, to
18     leave at the address the notice prescribed by subsection (e).
19         (2)  Mail to the registered qualified elector at the
20  address given on the registration card the notice prescribed by
21  subsection (e). The notice shall be sent as first-class mail and
22  shall contain on the outside a request to the postmaster to
23  forward it if the addressee does not reside at the address given
24  thereon.
25     (e)  Communication with commission.--The notice stipulated by
26  subsection (d) shall require the registered qualified elector to
27  communicate with the commission by a date designated by the
28  commission, which (date) shall be not less than ten days nor
29  more than 30 days from the service or mailing of the notice and,
30  in any case, not later than the 15th day preceding the election,
1  and satisfy the commission of qualifications as a qualified
2  elector. At the expiration of the time specified in the notice,
3  the commission shall cancel the registration of a person who has
4  not communicated with the commission and proved qualifications
5  as a qualified elector; however, if a registered qualified
6  elector who has been mailed the notice prescribed by this
7  subsection communicates with the commission claiming the right
8  to remain registered at the address to which the original notice
9  was mailed, the commission shall investigate and, if not
10  satisfied of the right of the qualified elector to remain
11  registered at the address, shall cancel the registration of the
12  elector. Every elector whose registration is canceled under this
13  section must register in the manner provided by this act in
14  order to be eligible to vote at any election.
15     (f)  Elector in military service.--The registration of a
16  person in military service shall not be canceled by reason of
17  the failure of the person to reside at the address appearing
18  upon the district register if the person resided at the address
19  on the date of entering military service.
20  Section 908.  Canvass of registered electors.
21     (a)  Verification.--The commission may, by individual
22  commissioners or by inspectors of registration, verify the
23  registration in an election district by visiting each building
24  from which an elector is registered and other buildings as the
25  commission deems necessary. The commission shall make a record
26  of the name and address of each person registered who is not
27  found to reside at the address from which the person is
28  registered or who, for any other reason, appears to be not
29  qualified to vote in the election district from which the person
30  is registered. The commission shall leave at the address of each
1  such person the notice prescribed by section 907(e).
2     (b)  Action.--At the expiration of the time specified in the
3  notice under section 907(e), the commission shall cancel or
4  suspend the registration of each person who has not communicated
5  with the commission and proved qualifications as an elector.
6     (c)  Special inspectors.--For the purpose of facilitating a
7  canvass, the commission may, when necessary, appoint special
8  inspectors of registration in number not exceeding double the
9  number of election districts which the commission determines to
10  canvass. They must be qualified electors of the county. They
11  shall be appointed without reference to residence in election
12  districts or to their political affiliations or beliefs. The
13  commission shall instruct each special inspector in discharging
14  duties.
15  Section 909.  Comparison and correction of registers.
16     Commencing 30 days prior to each election, the commission
17  shall compare and correct the general and district registers.
18  Section 910.  Petition to strike off names.
19     (a)  Initiation.--At any time not later than the tenth day
20  preceding an election, a qualified elector, including any
21  watcher and any registrar or inspector of registration, may
22  petition the commission to cancel or suspend the registration of
23  a registered qualified elector. The petition must set forth,
24  under oath or affirmation, all of the following:
25         (1)  Sufficient grounds for the cancellation or
26     suspension.
27         (2)  That:
28             (i)  notice of the time and place when the petition
29         would be presented has been given personally to the
30         registered qualified elector at least 24 hours prior to
1         the presentation of the petition; or
2             (ii)  the registered qualified elector could not be
3         found at the place given in the district register as
4         residence and the person in charge of that place, whose
5         name must be given in the petition, has declared that the
6         person was well acquainted with the names of all
7         individuals residing at the place and that the registered
8         qualified elector had never been or was no longer one of
9         them or that no such individual is residing at the
10         address.
11     (b)  Action.--Upon receipt of a petition under subsection
12  (a), the commission shall cancel or suspend the registration of
13  the qualified elector and amend accordingly the general and
14  district registers and other records affected unless the
15  qualified elector so registered appears and shows cause why this
16  action should not take place.
17  Section 911.  Failure to vote.
18     (a)  Check of electors.--By April 1 of each year except in a
19  year in which the commission conducts a check of electors under
20  section 907(c), the commission shall examine all of the district
21  registers.
22     (b)  Suspension.--A qualified elector who has been registered
23  for a period of at least two immediately preceding calendar
24  years but who is not recorded as having voted at an election
25  during that period is subject to suspension of registration in
26  accordance with the following procedure:
27         (1)  If a qualified elector who has been registered for a
28     period of at least two immediately preceding calendar years
29     is not recorded as having voted at an election during that
30     period, the commission shall send to the qualified elector by
1     mail, at the address appearing upon the registration card, a
2     notice setting forth that the records of the commission
3     indicate that the qualified elector has not voted during the
4     two immediately preceding calendar years and that the
5     qualified elector's registration will be canceled at the
6     expiration of 30 days from the date of mailing the notice
7     unless the qualified elector, within that period, files with
8     the commission, either personally or by mail, a written
9     request for reinstatement of registration or a removal notice
10     properly executed, setting forth the qualified elector's
11     place of residence and signed by the qualified elector.
12         (2)  Within 30 days from the date of mailing of the
13     notice under paragraph (1), the qualified elector must file
14     with the commission, either personally or by mail, a written
15     request for reinstatement of registration or a properly
16     executed removal notice, which sets forth the qualified
17     elector's place of residence and which is signed by the
18     qualified elector. The official registration application card
19     of a qualified elector who has registered by mail qualifies
20     as a request for reinstatement of registration or a removal
21     notice under this paragraph.
22         (3)  Within 30 days from the date of mailing of the
23     notice under paragraph (1), the commission shall cancel the
24     registration of a qualified elector who has not complied with
25     paragraph (2).
26     (c)  Effect.--Cancellation of registration under this section
27  shall not affect the right of a qualified elector to
28  subsequently register in the manner provided by this act.
29  Section 912.  Cancellation, removal and preservation of
30                 registration cards.
1     (a)  General rule.--If the registration of a qualified
2  elector is canceled for any cause, the commission shall mark on
3  the registration cards of the elector the word "canceled" and
4  the date and cause of cancellation and shall remove them from
5  the general and district registers. Each such card shall be kept
6  for five years. After five years, the commission may destroy it.
7     (b)  Nonessential records.--All records which are not
8  essential for maintaining the current status of a qualified
9  elector may be destroyed by the commission after three years.
10  Section 13.  Correction of errors in cancellation or suspension.
11     If the registration of a qualified elector has been canceled
12  or suspended through error, the qualified elector may petition
13  the commission for reinstatement of registration not later than
14  the tenth day preceding an election. After a hearing on the
15  application, if error on the part of the commission is proved,
16  the commission shall reinstate the registration.
17                             CHAPTER 11
18                       COMMISSION PROCEEDINGS
19  Section 1101.  Subpoenas and witness fees.
20     (a)  Authorization.--The commission may issue a subpoena.
21     (b)  Form and effect.--A subpoena under subsection (a) shall
22  be in substantially the same form and have the same force and
23  effect as a subpoena issued by a court of common pleas. The
24  commission shall have the benefit of the process of the
25  appropriate court of common pleas if necessary to enforce a
26  subpoena.
27     (c)  Benefit.--A subpoena may be issued as follows:
28         (1)  Upon the motion of the commission.
29         (2)  Upon motion of a party before the commission. A
30     subpoena under this paragraph is only valid for one day. It
1     must be renewed by 4 p.m. for the next day.
2     (d)  Fees.--
3         (1)  Witnesses subpoenaed under this section shall be
4     compensated under 42 Pa.C.S. ยง 5903 (relating to compensation
5     and expenses of witnesses).
6         (2)  Witnesses subpoenaed under subsection (c)(1) shall
7     be paid by commission funds.
8         (3)  Witnesses subpoenaed under subsection (c)(2) shall
9     be paid by the party. No subpoena shall be issued under
10     subsection (c)(2) until the party pays the commission a fee
11     of $25 for issuing the same and deposits with the commission
12     one day's witness fees for each witness to be summoned.
13         (4)  As soon as convenient after a hearing is concluded
14     or continued, the commission shall pay witnesses under
15     paragraphs (2) and (3). Unearned fees deposited under
16     paragraph (3) shall be refunded to the depositing party.
17         (5)  If the petition of the elector is sustained, the
18     commission shall pay to the elector all costs paid on the
19     elector's behalf.
20     (e)  Commission funds.--The commission shall pay over to the
21  county treasurer fees received under subsection (d)(3). The
22  accounts of the commission respecting payments under subsection
23  (d)(2) shall be subject to audit by the county controller.
24                             CHAPTER 13
25                          JUDICIAL REVIEW
26  Section 1301.  Court of common pleas.
27     (a)  Standing.--The following have standing to appeal an
28  action of a commission to the appropriate court of common pleas:
29         (1)  An individual whose claim for registration has been
30     denied.
1         (2)  An individual whose registration has been canceled
2     or suspended by the commission.
3         (3)  A qualified elector of a municipality whose rights
4     are impaired by any general order made by the commission.
5     (b)  Time.--An appeal under subsection (a) must be made by
6  the seventh day preceding an election.
7     (c)  Grounds.--The appeal must request relief and set forth
8  the grounds for relief.
9     (d)  Hearing.--Upon timely receipt of an appeal under this
10  section, the court shall conduct a hearing.
11     (e)  Order.--If the court finds that an injustice has been
12  done, it shall reverse or modify the ruling of the commission
13  and issue appropriate injunctive relief.
14     (f)  Costs.--
15         (1)  Except as provided in paragraph (2), the court may
16     award costs for the appeal to the prevailing party.
17         (2)  Costs may not be assessed against a commission or a
18     county.
19  Section 1302.  Commission duties.
20     In an appeal under section 1301, the registration commission
21  shall produce any petition, register or other record in its
22  custody relevant to the issue involved.
23                             CHAPTER 15
24                             PENALTIES
25  Section 1501.  Lawful orders.
26     A person who intentionally disobeys a lawful order of a
27  commission or a commissioner commits a misdemeanor of the third
28  degree and shall, upon conviction, be sentenced to pay a fine of
29  not more than $2,500.
30  Section 1502.  Registration.
1     (a)  Improper.--A registrar, commissioner or clerk who
2  knowingly registers or permits the registration of an individual
3  not lawfully entitled to be registered commits a misdemeanor of
4  the first degree and shall, upon conviction, be sentenced to pay
5  a fine of not more than $10,000 or to imprisonment for not more
6  than five years, or both.
7     (b)  Denial.--A registrar, commissioner or clerk who, without
8  reasonable cause, refuses to register an individual lawfully
9  entitled to be registered commits a misdemeanor of the first
10  degree and shall, upon conviction, be sentenced to pay a fine of
11  not more than $10,000 or to imprisonment for not more than five
12  years, or both.
13  Section 1503.  Application.
14     (a)  Prohibition.--An individual may not do any of the
15  following:
16         (1)  Apply for registration with knowledge or reason to
17     know that the individual is not entitled to registration.
18         (2)  Apply for a change of residence with knowledge or
19     reason to know that the individual is not entitled to the
20     change.
21         (3)  Declare as residence a place or address which the
22     individual knows is not the individual's legal residence.
23         (4)  Intentionally impersonate another in an application
24     for registration.
25     (b)  Penalty.--A person who violates subsection (a) commits a
26  misdemeanor of the first degree and shall, upon conviction, be
27  sentenced to pay a fine of not more than $10,000 or to
28  imprisonment for not more than five years, or both, and to
29  forfeit the right of suffrage for ten years.
30  Section 1504.  Altering registration.
1     (a)  Prohibition.--No registrar, commissioner, clerk or
2  assistant or commission employee may do any of the following:
3         (1)  Intentionally insert or permit to be inserted an
4     entry in a registration card without a proper application
5     under this act or without requiring the proper evidence of
6     the right of the applicant to be registered.
7         (2)  Intentionally materially alter a registration card
8     after the entries have been made. This paragraph does not
9     apply to an alteration pursuant to an order of a court of
10     common pleas or of the commission.
11     (b)  Penalty.--A person who violates subsection (a) commits a
12  misdemeanor of the first degree and shall, upon conviction, be
13  sentenced to pay a fine of not more than $10,000 or to
14  imprisonment for not more than five years, or both.
15  Section 1505.  Votes.
16     (a)  Prohibition.--An election officer may not do any of the
17  following:
18         (1)  Knowingly refuse the vote of a registered and
19     qualified elector.
20         (2)  Knowingly accept the vote of a person not registered
21     under this act. This paragraph does not apply to a person in
22     actual military service or a person having an order of court.
23         (3)  Knowingly receive a vote from a person falsely
24     claiming to be a registered voter.
25     (b)  Penalty.--A person who violates subsection (a) commits a
26  misdemeanor of the first degree and shall, upon conviction, be
27  sentenced to pay a fine of not more than $10,000 or to
28  imprisonment for not more than five years, or both.
29  Section 1506.  Duties under act.
30     Any commissioner, registrar, clerk, inspector of
1  registration, commission officer, commission assistant,
2  commission employee, individual, partnership or corporation that
3  intentionally delays, neglects or refuses to perform a duty
4  imposed by this act commits a misdemeanor of the second degree
5  and shall, upon conviction, be sentenced to pay a fine of not
6  more than $5,000 or to imprisonment for not more than two years,
7  or both.
8  Section 1507.  Official documents.
9     (a)  Prohibition.--A person may not do any of the following:
10         (1)  Intentionally insert or permit to be inserted a
11     material entry in any registration card, street list,
12     affidavit, petition, subpoena, certificate, report or other
13     record, authorized or required by this act to be made or
14     prepared for a purpose set forth in this act, which (entry)
15     is not in accordance with this act.
16         (2)  Intentionally materially alter or intentionally
17     destroy an entry which has been made in any registration
18     card, street list, affidavit, petition, subpoena,
19     certificate, report or other record, authorized or required
20     by this act to be made or prepared for a purpose set forth in
21     this act, which (alteration or destruction) is not in
22     accordance with this act.
23         (3)  Remove a record from lawful custody with the intent
24     to prevent the record from being used, inspected or copied.
25     (b)  Penalty.--A person who violates subsection (a) commits a
26  misdemeanor of the first degree and shall, upon conviction, be
27  sentenced to pay a fine of not more than $6,000 or to
28  imprisonment for not more than three years, or both.
29  Section 1508.  Withholding information.
30     A person who intentionally refuses to furnish to a
1  commissioner or an inspector of registration information or
2  documents which the commissioner or inspector is authorized to
3  have under this act commits a misdemeanor of the second degree
4  and shall, upon conviction, be sentenced to pay a fine of not
5  more than $5,000 or to imprisonment for not more than two years,
6  or both.
7  Section 1509.  Law enforcement assistance.
8     A law enforcement officer who, upon demand of any
9  commissioner or inspector of registration, fails to render
10  demanded assistance in the maintenance of peace and in the
11  making of arrests without warrant as provided in this act or who
12  intentionally hinders or attempts to hinder any commissioner or
13  inspector of registration in the performance of a duty commits a
14  misdemeanor of the second degree and shall, upon conviction, be
15  sentenced to pay a fine of not more than $5,000 or to
16  imprisonment for not more than two years, or both.
17  Section 1510.  Interference.
18     A person who intentionally interferes with any other person
19  in the performance of any act or duty authorized or imposed by
20  this act commits a misdemeanor of the second degree and shall,
21  upon conviction, be sentenced to pay a fine of not more than
22  $5,000 or to imprisonment for not more than two years, or both.
23  Section 1511.  Preventing registration.
24     (a)  Prohibition.--No person may do any of the following:
25         (1)  Knowingly and intentionally prevent an individual
26     from being registered.
27         (2)  By coercion, threats of bodily injury or
28     intimidation, intentionally prevent or attempt to prevent an
29     individual from being registered or from changing political
30     enrollment in accordance with the provisions of this act.
1         (3)  Intentionally give or promise or offer to give money
2     or goods to an individual as an inducement for the individual
3     to enroll in a particular party or to change political
4     enrollment.
5         (4)  Prevent a record from being used, inspected or
6     copied.
7     (b)  Penalty.--A person who violates subsection (a) commits a
8  misdemeanor of the first degree and shall, upon conviction, be
9  sentenced to pay a fine of not more than $10,000 or to
10  imprisonment for not more than five years, or both.
11  Section 1512.  Approval of registration.
12     (a)  Prohibition.--A person may not do any of the following:
13         (1)  Intentionally alter a party designation on a
14     registration card under section 528(c).
15         (2)  Intentionally fail to make a transmission under
16     section 528(d).
17     (b)  Penalty.--A person who violates subsection (a) commits a
18  misdemeanor of the third degree and shall, upon conviction, be
19  sentenced to pay a fine of $2,500 or to imprisonment for not
20  more than one year, or both.
21  Section 1513.  Crimes Code.
22     The provisions of 18 Pa.C.S. ยงยง 4902 (relating to perjury),
23  4903 (relating to false swearing) and 4904 (relating to unsworn
24  falsification to authorities) apply to violations of this act.

17  Section 5101.  Application for absentee ballot.
18     (a)  General rule.--Notwithstanding the provisions of this
19  act or the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the
20  Pennsylvania Election Code, the following persons may make
21  application for an absentee ballot by sending a letter or other

15  contained on the form prescribed by the secretary, which was
16  prepared or distributed by a political party and signed by a
17  qualified elector, shall be deemed valid and shall be reviewed
18  and processed by the county board of elections in the same
19  manner as applications on forms prescribed by the secretary and
20  supplied by county boards of elections.
